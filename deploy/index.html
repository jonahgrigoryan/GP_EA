Understanding Your Condition

What is a Chronic Wound?

A chronic wound is defined as a wound that fails to progress through the normal stages of healing within an expected timeframe, typically four to six weeks. These wounds often become stuck in the inflammatory phase and require specialized intervention to restart the healing process.

Chronic wounds can develop from various underlying conditions including diabetes, poor circulation, prolonged pressure, or compromised immune function. Without proper treatment, these wounds can lead to serious complications including infection, tissue damage, and decreased quality of life.

At Advanced Wound Care & Solutions, we work collaboratively with your primary care provider to address both the wound itself and the underlying factors that may be impeding your healing. This team-based approach ensures you receive comprehensive care that targets the root causes while promoting optimal wound recovery.

Early intervention is key. If you have a wound that hasn't shown improvement within 2-3 weeks, contact us for a comprehensive evaluation.

Innovative Healing

Advanced Approaches

Standard wound care methods often fall short for chronic wounds. We utilize advanced regenerative therapies that work at the cellular level, providing your body with the resources it needs to heal more effectively.

Cellular Regeneration Therapy

Your body contains natural repair mechanisms that can be harnessed to heal damaged tissue. When applied to a wound, these cellular therapies work by:

  • Stimulating new cell development to rebuild damaged areas
  • Decreasing inflammation that can impede the healing process
  • Enhancing circulation to deliver essential nutrients and oxygen
Ideal for: Deep wounds, diabetic ulcers, and wounds with compromised blood flow

Amniotic Tissue Grafts

Sourced from donated placental tissue, amniotic grafts serve as a highly effective biological wound covering. These grafts offer:

  • Natural growth factors that activate your body's healing response
  • A protective layer that guards against infection and fluid loss
  • Anti-inflammatory benefits that help minimize discomfort and swelling
Ideal for: Venous ulcers, pressure injuries, and burn wounds

Biologic Therapies

State-of-the-art cellular treatments and growth factor applications engineered for the most challenging wound cases:

  • Accelerating tissue regeneration to restore damaged skin
  • Targeting inflammatory responses that delay wound closure
  • Optimizing vascular supply for improved healing conditions
Ideal for: Complex wounds, diabetic foot ulcers, and circulation-impaired wounds
